So, what it is all about? In the middle of the next year (i.e. 2020) it will be ten years since I got mine M.Sc. diploma in Computer Science and started1 my professional career as a software developer. Hence, I decided it is a good time to stop for a moment and take a look back at the last ten years. And that’s how this weblog came to be.

Actually it’s one of two reasons to put this site on the net. The second one is a more practical reason. It happened to me a few times to solve the same problem more than once. Due to the fact I couldn’t recall anything from memory and most external web sources were blocked by security of this particular company. Very frustrating and counterproductive.

Most of my professional career I’ve spent dealing with Tibco BusinessWorks BW 5. For those of you who don’t know BW 5 is part of a complete product stack for building enterprise grade EAI/ESB platforms. It’s great and mature software directed at bigish enterprises, so it’s not very popular and almost unheard of outside the enterprise world. Also what’s more important BW 5 is sort of VPL which by defition is very often loathed by normal programmers. BW itself is wholly written in Java and requries JRE to run. From software develoepr perspective there is one crucial thing I have to mention, that makes this product so tempting to use. BW take ideas of reusability and componentization to the extreme which speeds up development by order of magnitude in comparision to standard programming languages. The trade-off is (there’s always one) that you can’t build, let’s say webapps or CLI apps in TIBCO. Main puropse of BW (at low level) is to provide a tool that can be used to build tiny apps that consume or provide APIs which EAI is all about. One last thing if you’re not convinced about 10x speed of developement, let me add it’s not uncommon (telling from my experience) that BW is often last resort in delivering functionalites that can’t be delivered by other corporate systems (CRMs, Billings and such) in reasonable timeline for given project. Sometimes it goes as far as implementing business logic on EAI. Yeah, I know it’s terrible in terms of architecture but that’s whole different story.

Least of my career I’ve spent working with Java and other related techs. However, since the begining of 2019 I play with Spring Boot and Docker. I am very happy about it and I hope it will stay this way for a while.
